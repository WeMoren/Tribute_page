Albert Einstein

Theoretical physicist and nobel laureate

March 14, 1879 - April 18, 1955

Biography

Early/later life, Personal life and Legacy

  • Born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Kingdom of Wuttermberg, German Empire
  • Grew up in middle class Jewish family with parents,Hermann and Pauline Einstein
  • Showed curiocity and passion for learning from an early age
  • Married Mileva Maric (1903 - 1919) and Elsa Lowenthal (1919 - 1936)
  • Moved to the United States (1933) and became a U.S. citizen (1940)

    • Education and Career

  • Studied physics at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic University (1896 - 1900)
  • Worked as a patent clerk in Bern, Switzerland (1902 - 1909)
  • Became a theoretical physics professor at the university of Zurich (1911 - 1915)
  • Developed The Theory of Relativity (1905 and 1915)
  • Advocated for peace, civil rights and nuclear disarmament
  • Pass away on April 18, 1955 in Princeton, New Jersey

Achievement

Achievements and Awards

  • Nobel Prize in physics (1921) for explaining Photoelectric Efffect
  • Copley Medal (1925) from the Royal Society
  • Time Magazine's Person of the Century
